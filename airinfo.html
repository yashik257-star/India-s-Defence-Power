INDIAN AIR FORCE

The Indian Air Force (IAF) is the air arm of the Indian Armed Forces. Its primary mission is to secure Indian airspace and to conduct aerial warfare during armed conflicts. It was officially established on 8 October 1932 as an auxiliary air force of the British India which honoured India's aviation during World War II.

The President India holds the rank of Supreme Commander of the IAF. The Chief of the Air Staff, an air chief marshal, is a four-star officer and is responsible for the bulk of operational command of the Air Force. There is never more than one serving ACM at any given time in the IAF.



  • Top Leadership
    • Chief of the Air Staff (CAS): Head of the entire air force, responsible for strategy and policy.
    • Vice Chief of the Air Staff (VCAS):Assists the CAS and oversees daily operations.
    • Deputy Chiefs of the Air Staff (DCAS):Support the VCAS in specific areas like operations, training, maintenance.
    • Principal Staff Officers (PSOs):Head major branches like Personnel, Maintenance, Administration, and Operations.
  • Command Structure
    • Commands:The force is divided into geographical or functional commands (e.g., Western, Eastern, Training, Maintenance).
    • Air Officer Commanding-in-Chief (AOC-in-C):An Air Marshal leads each Command.
  • Field Units
    • Stations/Bases:Air bases are where squadrons are located.
    • Wings:A formation within a command, usually comprising 2-3 squadrons and support units.
    • Squadrons:The primary flying units, led by a Wing Commander or Group Captain.
    • Flights:Subdivisions of a squadron, led by a Squadron Leader.
    • Sections:The smallest unit, often 3 aircraft, led by a Flight Lieutenant.
