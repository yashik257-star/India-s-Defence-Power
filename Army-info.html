Information about Indian Army

The Indian Army is the world's largest volunteer land force, a branch of the Indian Armed Forces,tasked with protecting India from external/internal threats, maintainingsecurity, and aiding during disasters, led by the Chief of Army Staff the President, with motto "Service Before Self",celebrating its history on Army Day on "15 January" and known for its discipline, diversity, and global peacekeeping..



7 Commands and Headquaters of Indian Army

Northern Command: Udhampur, Jammu & Kashmir (Focuses on northern borders).
Western Command:Chandimandir, Haryana (Manages western borders).
Eastern Command: Kolkata, West Bengal (Covers eastern borders, Northeast).
Southern Command:Pune, Maharashtra (Guards southern & coastal areas).
Central Command: Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh (Strategic reserve, central India).
South Western Command: Jaipur, Rajasthan (Desert sector security).
Army Training Command (ARTRAC):Shimla, Himachal Pradesh (Oversees training and professional development)
